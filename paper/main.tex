\begin{abstract}
We present an end-to-end university timetabling system that schedules lectures, tutorials, and labs over a fixed weekly grid.
The solver combines a CP-SAT feasibility model (OR-Tools), two room-assignment modes (integrated CP rooming and fast greedy rooming), and an optional local-search post-improver.
The implementation also includes a phased solve pipeline for UI use: feasibility-first search under a time budget followed by iterative improvement rounds.
On generated scenarios (\texttt{small\_demo}, \texttt{block\_profs}, \texttt{mixed\_large}, \texttt{target\_case}), 16/16 runs were feasible under retry/fallback settings.
For larger scenarios, requesting fast greedy rooming was consistently faster than requesting strict CP rooming (median speedup about \(1.8\times\) to \(2.1\times\)).
We provide model details, system architecture, reproducible experiment commands, and implementation-grounded limitations.
\end{abstract}
