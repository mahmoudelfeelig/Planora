\begin{abstract}
We present an end-to-end university timetabling system that schedules lectures, tutorials, and labs over a fixed weekly grid.
The solver combines a CP-SAT feasibility model (OR-Tools), two room-assignment modes (integrated CP rooming and fast greedy rooming), and an optional local-search post-improver.
The implementation also includes a phased solve pipeline for UI use: feasibility-first search under a time budget followed by iterative improvement rounds.
On generated scenarios (\texttt{small\_demo}, \texttt{block\_profs}, \texttt{mixed\_large}, \texttt{target\_case}),
24/24 reliability runs are feasible under retry/fallback settings.
For larger scenarios, requested fast greedy rooming is consistently faster than requested strict CP rooming
(about \(1.59\times\) to \(1.98\times\) in this batch).
Local-search ablations show additional quality gains after feasibility (up to \(1.13\%\) median penalty reduction on \texttt{block\_profs}).
We provide model details, system architecture, release-ready reproducibility tooling, and implementation-grounded limitations.
\end{abstract}
