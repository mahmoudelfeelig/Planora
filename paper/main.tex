\begin{abstract}
University course timetabling must satisfy strict feasibility requirements while still producing practical weekly schedules for students and staff.
This paper presents a complete scheduling approach for lectures, tutorials, and labs under realistic university constraints, including room compatibility, staff availability, and program/group structure.
Our method first constructs a conflict-free timetable, then improves quality through a post-optimization stage that targets soft preferences such as free-day patterns and compactness.
Across benchmark-style generated scenarios (\texttt{small\_demo}, \texttt{block\_profs}, \texttt{mixed\_large}, \texttt{target\_case}), reliability experiments obtain feasible schedules in all 24/24 runs with retry/fallback enabled.
For larger instances, fast greedy room assignment is consistently faster than strict integrated room assignment (approximately \(1.59\times\) to \(1.98\times\) in our batch), while local improvement yields additional quality gains after feasibility (up to \(1.13\%\) median penalty reduction on \texttt{block\_profs}).
The paper focuses on the mathematical model, algorithmic decisions, and reproducible evaluation protocol needed to deploy the system in practice.
\end{abstract}
