\begin{abstract}
University course timetabling is the task of deciding when and where classes should take place while respecting the needs of students, instructors, and rooms.
It is difficult because many requirements must be satisfied at the same time, and even small conflicts can make a timetable unusable in practice.
This paper presents a scheduling framework for lectures, tutorials, and laboratories in realistic academic settings.
The approach separates the problem into two stages: it first builds a timetable that satisfies the core operational constraints, then it improves the result according to softer quality goals such as more balanced and more practical weekly schedules.
We describe the problem formulation, the main optimization strategy, and a reproducible evaluation process.
The results show that the framework can reliably generate valid timetables and then refine them further without sacrificing feasibility, making it suitable as a practical foundation for academic scheduling tools.
\end{abstract}
