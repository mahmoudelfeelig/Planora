\begin{abstract}
We present a university timetabling system that schedules lectures, tutorials, and labs over a fixed weekly grid.
Our approach combines a constraint-programming feasibility model solved with Google's OR-Tools CP-SAT solver, optional soft-constraint optimization, and a post-processing local-search improver.
We describe the mathematical model, key hard and soft constraints, and the overall software architecture.
Experimental results are reported on generated instances and, optionally, public benchmarks.
\end{abstract}
