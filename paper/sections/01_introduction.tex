This paper describes a university timetabling system that schedules academic activities on a fixed weekly grid.
The primary goal is feasibility under hard constraints (no conflicts, availability, room eligibility), with secondary quality objectives such as reducing gaps and improving schedule stability across weeks.

Our contributions are:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item A CP-SAT feasibility model with optional weighted soft penalties, implemented in OR-Tools CP-SAT~\cite{cpsatlp,ortools,ortools_cite,perron_didier_gay_cpsatlp_2023}.
  \item A room-assignment strategy supporting both integrated CP rooming and a faster post-processing assignment.
  \item A local-search improver that reduces a composite penalty while maintaining feasibility.
  \item An end-to-end software system (CLI and desktop UI) with exports (DOCX/CSV/ICS/PDF) and reproducibility hooks.
\end{itemize}

All non-trivial solver claims in this paper are tied directly to the implementation and/or cited references.
Experimental results are produced by running the released code and are not inferred.
